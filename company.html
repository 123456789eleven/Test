Why the circle matters — each division individually competes against specialists that are often dramatically larger — Gallagher in consulting, WEX/Alight in administration, ADP/Paychex in payroll, CAPTRUST/OneDigital in retirement. Almost none of those specialists connect all four under one client relationship and one technology platform the way Kelly does. If there's a strategic edge here, it's integration, not any single division's scale — and OneDigital (health + wealth convergence) is the one competitor pursuing the same idea, just at a much larger scale.

    Where "enrollment" and "reconciliation" actually sit

    Enrollment isn't one thing — it likely spans three verticals: initial enrollment setup during onboarding (Construct), COBRA elections specifically (Connect), and ongoing annual open enrollment support (Serve). Worth confirming whether Advantage even splits it that way, or handles it as one continuous function.

    Reconciliation (eligibility, billing) most plausibly sits in Connect — it's the same "keep two systems in sync" work as COBRA admin — but could also be a Serve function if it's framed as client-facing account maintenance rather than backend data work.

    SWOT

    The circle and the competitor data, synthesized into a strategic read — not a description of what Kelly is, but a read on the position it's actually in.

    Strengths

    • Integration across all four divisions — one client relationship instead of four vendors
    • Total Benefits Solution® / KTBSonline is genuinely in-house, not licensed — full control over the product
    • 50 years of family stability and reputation; 23 consecutive years as MD's #1 benefits administrator
    • Advantage's broker-technology licensing gives it a second revenue line beyond direct clients

    Weaknesses

    • Strategies is a regional player next to national giants — Gallagher alone is ~55× its estimated revenue
    • Advisory isn't fully independent — it runs under Global Retirement Partners/HUB's regulatory umbrella
    • No public financials — headcount and scale are third-party estimates even for someone working there
    • Limited brand visibility outside the Mid-Atlantic region

    Opportunities

    • Push the health + wealth convergence further — OneDigital is proving the model at scale
    • Grow Advantage's tech-licensing line as a distinct product, not just a byproduct
    • Continue the Gettysburg-style acquisition pattern to grow Strategies inorganically
    • Use the 50th anniversary as a real visibility moment beyond the home region

    Threats

    • PE-backed roll-ups (Navia, OneDigital, HUB) have more acquisition capital than a private family firm
    • Industry consolidation could squeeze independent regional players from both ends
    • A new CTO and 70-person tech team is a bet — if it underdelivers, competitors' platforms may pull ahead
    • Client tech expectations keep rising (consumer-grade UX), raising the cost of staying competitive
